ABOUT

Many christians are familiar with the phrase What Would Jesus Do (WWJD), a reminder to pattern our lives after Jesus. However, the church today has lost sight of the Gospel and instead put too much focus on our individual performance in DOING rather than BELIEVING. In order to respond to WWJD we must be united in Christ. Our good works bear witness to the work of the Spirit in our lives, but are not sufficient in themselves for evangelistic purposes. What Jesus has done is what we rest upon for salvation.
His finished work on the cross.

Today we see an emphasis on What Would Jesus Do over What Has Jesus Done.
